Clery Compliance

A great safety program is Clery compliance.

When officers are focused on reducing incidents — not entering data — compliance follows naturally. Legio is built around that idea.

See It in Action

The Real Problem

Too much to record. Too little time to act.

Campus security teams are buried in data entry. Every form, every log, every report is time an officer isn't spending on presence, prevention, or response.

The Legio Principle

Recording is native to the job, not in addition to it.

When the act of doing the job generates the record automatically, compliance stops being a workload and starts being a measure of good work.

The Result

Teams focused on reducing incidents, not reporting them.

Clery compliance becomes the proof that your program is working — not the effort that proves it.

The Problem

The burden isn't Clery. It's everything around it.

The Clery Act itself is straightforward. The problem is that the software built to support it treats documentation as the primary job — leaving officers spending more time recording work than doing it.

A team focused on data entry is a team not focused on safety.

01

Data entry crowds out real work

When recording is treated as a separate task, it competes with the job itself. Officers choose between documenting well and responding well — they shouldn't have to choose.

02

Compliance becomes a project, not a program

When data entry and compliance are separate workstreams, Clery becomes something you scramble to produce — not something your program naturally generates.

03

The wrong metric drives the team

If the measure of success is forms completed, the team optimizes for forms. Legio is built around a different measure: incidents reduced.

The Legio Approach

Make doing the job the same as recording it.

Legio is designed so that the act of responding to an incident, completing a patrol, or managing a situation generates the record automatically. Officers don't enter data — they do their jobs, and the data follows.

When that's true, Clery compliance isn't a workload. It's a scoreboard.

🎯

Capture is embedded in the workflow

Data is collected at the moment of action — not entered afterward. The form follows the officer, not the other way around.

📍

Classification happens automatically

Clery geography, incident categories, and reportability are determined by the system — not by manual review after the fact.

📊

Compliance is a live measure, not an annual report

Your Clery record is current every day of the year. The Annual Security Report is a formatted view of work already done — not a project you build from scratch.

The Difference

What changes when recording is native to the job.

Today

Officers are measured on data entry. Safety is secondary.

Respond to an incident, then return to the office to document it

Complete a patrol, then log it manually after the fact

Review incidents separately to determine Clery reportability

Assemble compliance reports from disparate records at year-end

Leadership measures activity by forms completed, not outcomes delivered

With Legio

Officers are measured on outcomes. Compliance follows.

Respond to an incident — the record is generated as part of the response

Complete a patrol — the log is captured natively through the platform

Clery classification is determined at the time of the event, not after

Compliance data is current every day, not assembled once a year

Leadership measures what matters: incidents reduced, response improved

How It Works

The job generates the record.

Legio is designed around a simple idea: if recording is native to the work, officers do more of the work — and compliance is what you get when the work is done well.

01

Officers work in the platform

Dispatch, patrol, incident response — all managed through Legio. The platform is the job, not an add-on to it. Records are generated as officers work, not entered separately.

02

Clery classification is automatic

Every event is classified against Clery requirements in real time — geography, category, reportability — without a manual review step. The system knows the rules so your officers don't have to apply them.

03

Compliance is always current

Your Clery record is live. The Annual Security Report is ready whenever you need it — because the work that generates it never stopped. October is just the day you publish.
