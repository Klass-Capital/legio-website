The Problem

Three ways traditional incident software fails your team.

Every incident management system built in the last 20 years was designed around the same assumption: officers should fill out forms. That assumption is wrong — and the failures that follow from it are predictable.

01

Data loss at intake

Officers simplify narratives to fit forms. The smell of alcohol, a subject's demeanor, the exact sequence of events — context that matters gets discarded because there's no dropdown for it.

Rich context → lost forever

02

Delayed reporting

The cognitive burden of form-filling means reports get written hours later from memory — or not at all. An incident that isn't documented promptly is an investigation that starts cold.

40% of minor incidents unreported

03

Siloed intelligence

Related incidents are never linked because systems can only match on exact field values. Ten thefts with the same MO across buildings go unnoticed because nothing connects them.

Patterns invisible until it's too late

Link Analysis

Finding connections it would be impossible to find manually.

Scanning incidents
Pattern detected. 6 bike thefts — evening timing, no forced entry, same MO. Confidence 91%.

Behavioral signature, not field matching

The engine analyzes MO, timing patterns, target selection, and suspect details — not just incident category. Six thefts with the same approach get flagged. Six random thefts don't.

Frequency-adjusted confidence scoring

Common incidents are penalized so they don't generate false clusters. Confidence only rises when the behavioral details are genuinely distinctive — a specific MO, recurring description, or timing pattern that defies coincidence.

How It Works

The model is inverted. The officer narrates. The system structures.

Every traditional system asks officers to translate what happened into fields and dropdowns. Legio asks officers to describe what happened — and does the translation itself.

01

Narrative-first intake

The officer speaks or types what happened — in plain language. The AI extracts every structured field: incident type, persons involved, location, timeline, property, demeanor. Confirmation takes 15–30 seconds, not 15–30 minutes.

02

Zero rekey from any source

If it's already been captured digitally — an email, a radio transcript, an alarm event, a witness statement PDF, a camera still — Legio ingests it and extracts the data. Officers never type something twice.

03

AI as active investigator

The system identifies missing information and proactively fills the gap — prompting the officer, sending follow-up questions to the original reporter, or flagging for a supervisor. It doesn't wait for a human to notice a hole.

04

Behavioral link detection

Every incident is vectorized by behavioral signature and compared against the full incident database. Related cases surface automatically — with confidence scores, shared MO details, and a clear reason for the connection.
