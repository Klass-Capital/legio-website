Policy Management

Stop hunting for signatures.

IACLEA accreditation requires a written directive for virtually every operational function. Most institutions can't prove compliance without weeks of manual assembly. Legio changes that.

IACLEA Standards

100+

Individual written directives required across 17 chapters — each with specific elements, review schedules, and acknowledgment requirements.

The Reality

Word docs. Shared drives. Binders.

That's where most campus security policies live today — with no system tracking what exists, what's current, or who's seen it.

The Gap

No platform connects policies to operations.

General tools manage documents. Legio manages policies in the context of the operations they govern.

The Problem

Accreditation shouldn't be a fire drill.

When assessors arrive, the scramble begins. Weeks of cross-referencing standards to documents, hunting down signatures, and rebuilding paper trails that should have been continuous.

01

No single source of truth

Policies are scattered across shared drives and institutional memory. There's no central record of what exists, what's been reviewed, or who approved it.

02

Policies disconnected from operations

The policy that governs your patrol procedure lives in a separate system from your patrol data. There's no linkage — and accreditors notice.

03

Compliance is reactive, not continuous

Without ongoing tracking, every accreditation cycle is a manual rebuild. Weeks of effort that should have been minutes.
