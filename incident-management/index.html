The Problem

Three ways traditional incident software fails your team.

Every incident management system built in the last 20 years was designed around one assumption: officers should fill out forms. That assumption is wrong.

Data loss at intake

Officers simplify narratives to fit forms. The smell of alcohol, a subject's demeanor, the exact sequence of events — context that matters gets discarded because there's no dropdown for it.

Rich context → lost forever

Manual at every step

Once an incident is filed, someone has to route it, notify stakeholders, escalate if needed, track follow-ups, and close it out. Every step depends on a person remembering to do it — and every missed step is a gap in your record.

Process runs on memory

Siloed intelligence

Related incidents are never linked because systems can only match on exact field values. A serial pattern across buildings with slightly different descriptions? Invisible to every legacy platform.

Patterns go undetected

How It Works

From scene to closed record. No forms.

Legio inverts the incident workflow. The officer narrates what happened. The system does everything else.

Narrative intake

The officer speaks or types what happened. No dropdowns, no structured entry screens. The platform extracts every data element — people, locations, times, incident type, severity.

Zero re-entry

Every element permeates everywhere it needs to go: the incident record, daily activity report, compliance datasets. Enter once, propagate everywhere.

Intelligent follow-through

Legio doesn't just store data — it works it. Link analysis runs continuously. Gaps generate follow-up prompts. An active partner, not a filing cabinet.
