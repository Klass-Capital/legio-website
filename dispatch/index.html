The problem

Every call for service creates work that has nothing to do with the response.

Log the call. Switch systems to check availability. Assign the officer. Switch again to communicate. Log the response. Enter the outcome. Legacy dispatch is a series of handoffs between tools — and most of that friction is invisible until it slows something down.

The dispatcher's job is to move resources to the right place. Every click that isn't that is a tax on response time.

Multiple systems, one call
Intake in one place, communication in another, outcome in a third. Fragmented across tools that don't talk to each other.
Re-entry at every step
What the dispatcher logs doesn't become the incident record. Someone re-enters the same facts in a different system.
No visibility without a call
Supervisors find out what happened when someone tells them — not while it's happening.
